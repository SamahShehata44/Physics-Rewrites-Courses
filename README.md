# ⚛️ Physics Course Rewrites & Synthesized Notes

Welcome to my collection of comprehensive course rewrites. These notes are designed to bridge the gap between lecture material and deep conceptual understanding through expanded derivations and detailed summaries.

---

## 📗 1. Fundamentals of Physics I (Yale PHYS 200)
**Instructor:** Prof. Ramamurti Shankar  
**File:** [Fundamentals_of_Physics_I.pdf](./Fundamentals_of_Physics_I.pdf)

### 🖋️ Why this Rewrite?
While the original Yale lectures are world-class, these notes are designed to:
*   **Bridge the Gaps:** Provide more step-by-step detail in complex mathematical derivations.
*   **Synthesize Concepts:** Group related ideas from different lectures into cohesive summaries.
*   **Clarification:** Rewrite difficult concepts in plain language for better student intuition.

### 📚 Topics Covered
*   **Newtonian Mechanics:** Kinematics, Dynamics, Energy, and Momentum.
*   **Rotational Dynamics:** Torque, Moment of Inertia, and Rolling Motion.
*   **Special Relativity:** Lorentz Transformations and Four-Vectors.
*   **Thermodynamics:** Heat, Entropy, and the Laws of Thermodynamics.

## ☢️ 2. Introduction to Nuclear and Particle Physics (MIT 8.701)
**Instructor:** Prof. Markus Klute  
**File:** [Introduction_To_Nuclear_and_particle_physics.pdf](./Introduction_To_Nuclear_and_particle_physics.pdf)

### 🖋️ Why this Rewrite?
These notes focus on providing "Important Notes" sidebars that explain *why* specific discoveries changed our understanding of the universe.

### 🔬 Course Highlights
*   **The Standard Model:** QED (Feynman diagrams) and QCD (Quarks and Gluons).
*   **Higgs Physics:** Spontaneous symmetry breaking and mass generation.
*   **Nuclear Physics:** Fission, fusion, and stellar nucleosynthesis.
*   **Detection Technology:** Calorimetry, Cherenkov radiation, and accelerators.
